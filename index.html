Module 1: Reading the Soup (METAR)

A METAR is a report of actual weather observed at an airport. In Europe, we use Hectopascals (QNH) and Meters for visibility.

EGLC 151020Z AUTO 26015G25KT 0800 R27/1200D FG OVC001 08/08 Q1002

Visual Key: The Sky Layers

Clouds are measured in "Oktas" (eighths of the sky). Anything BKN (Broken) or OVC (Overcast) is considered a "Ceiling."

CodeMeaningPilot's Perspective
FG / BRFog / MistFG < 1000m visibility; BR >= 1000m.
VV001Vertical VisibilitySky obscured; you can only see 100ft up.
R27/1200Runway Visual RangeExactly what a pilot sees down the runway.

Module 2: Dangerous Minima (INN, FNC, CMF)

Airports like Innsbruck (INN) or Madeira (FNC) require special training because the weather changes instantly.

Mountain Obscuration: A METAR might say SCT030, but those "scattered" clouds could be sitting right on a mountain peak you need to fly past!
